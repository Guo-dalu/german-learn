## Language Notes

- German family terms have **natural gender** matching the person: der Vater, die Mutter — no surprises here. But **das Kind** (child) and **das Baby** are neuter regardless of the child's gender.
- **Geschwister** (siblings) is always plural with no singular form — you can't say *ein Geschwister*. To specify: *ein Bruder*, *eine Schwester*.
- Possessives inflect by gender of the noun, not the owner: *mein Vater* (m), *meine Mutter* (f), *mein Kind* (n) — the owner being male or female doesn't change anything.
- **Wie heißt du?** vs **Wie alt bist du?** — both are essential for talking about family. Ages use *sein*: *Mein Bruder ist zwölf Jahre alt*, not *hat*.
